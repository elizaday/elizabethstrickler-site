About

I started at Boston College studying philosophy with Jesuit priests who taught me to see patterns across math, science, culture, music, and art as one interconnected system. That way of thinking has shaped everything since.

I came to Georgia State to teach high school math and fell in love with computer science just as the internet was beginning. I helped build the university's internet infrastructure, then joined MindSpring as employee #40 and its first female software engineer. I led their web hosting division during the early days of the commercial internet.

I returned to GSU to run the Kodak Cineon film system, earned an MFA in digital filmmaking, and started making short films and games. Eventually I became faculty, created the media entrepreneurship program, co-founded the Creative Media Industries Institute, and got jointly appointed in the College of Business.

Now I work as Director of Media Innovation at CMII, consult for Coca-Cola on AI media strategy, and teach courses on AI in the creative industries, immersive experience design, and media ventures.

BA Philosophy, Boston College. BS Computer Science, Georgia State. MFA Digital Filmmaking & Art, Georgia State. Program Chair, Immersive Experience Alliance. Fulbright Project Director.
